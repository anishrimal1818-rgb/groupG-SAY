Breathing Exercises

Simple yet powerful techniques to calm your mind and body in minutes.

Breathe In...

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

How to Practice:

  1. Find a comfortable seated position
  2. Exhale completely through your mouth
  3. Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds
  4. Hold your breath for 7 seconds
  5. Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 seconds
  6. Repeat for 4 cycles

Benefits: Reduces anxiety, helps with sleep, calms the nervous system.

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

This technique is used by Navy SEALs to stay calm under pressure.

How to Practice:

  1. Exhale to a count of 4
  2. Inhale to a count of 4
  3. Hold your breath for 4 seconds
  4. Exhale to a count of 4
  5. Repeat for 5-10 cycles

Tip: Visualize tracing a box as you breathe for better focus.

5-Minute Mindfulness

Quick mindfulness exercises that fit into even the busiest day.

Body Scan Meditation

Release tension from head to toe with this guided body awareness practice.

  1. Close your eyes and take 3 deep breaths
  2. Focus attention on your feet - notice sensations
  3. Slowly move attention up through legs, torso, arms
  4. End by relaxing your face and jaw

Grounding Exercise (5-4-3-2-1)

When anxiety strikes, reconnect with the present using your senses.

  1. Name 5 things you can see
  2. Name 4 things you can touch
  3. Name 3 things you can hear
  4. Name 2 things you can smell
  5. Name 1 thing you can taste

Loving-Kindness Meditation

Cultivate compassion for yourself and others through this gentle practice.

  1. Visualize someone you love
  2. Repeat: "May you be happy, may you be healthy"
  3. Include yourself: "May I be happy, may I be healthy"
  4. Extend to neutral people, then difficult relationships

Sleep Improvement Tips

Better sleep leads to better mental health. Try these evidence-based strategies.

Create a Sleep Sanctuary

Your bedroom should be a haven for rest. Keep it cool (65-68°F), dark, and quiet. Remove work materials and screens from the space.

Consistent Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even weekends. This helps regulate your body's internal clock for better sleep quality.

Limit Screen Time

Avoid screens 1-2 hours before bed. The blue light disrupts melatonin production. Try reading, stretching, or gentle music instead.
